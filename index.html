Global Internet Speed vs. Australia Comparisons (2017 - 2024)

Fixed Broadband download speed per country

Functionalities: Year selection - Countries are clickable

This map shows the average fixed-broadband download speeds by country from 2017 to 2024 to reveal where the world is fast, middling or lagging. In 2024, the highest speeds concentrate in some gulf countries like United Arab Emirates or Saudi Arabia, parts of East/Southeast Asia in Thailand and advanced European economies like France, Denmark and Spain.

Some countries are among the top one's during all years. North America, Canada and some european countries remained fast, reflecting a good infrastructure. Australia sits around the global middle and sometimes lower tier.

GDP per capita vs. fixed broadband speed (2017–2024)

Functionalities: Highlight Australia
  • Richer economies generally reflect a faster fixed-broadband that shows a clear positive association.

  • Some places with lower GDP still perform well that shows local policy and investment matter.

  • Across 2017–2024, speeds rise almost everywhere, especially after 2020.

  • Australia’s cluster performance is steady, not wildly swinging.

  • Australia shows a significantly lower speed in latest years than the countries with a similar income closer to Australia in earlier years.
    (eg. Au 71.9 Mbps in 2024 vs USA 75 Mbps in 2017)

Top 20 countries per year vs. Australia

Functionalities: Year selection

From 2017–2024, Singapore and Hong Kong showing a high speed repeatedly and stay over the top while other countries experience more rapid changes.

United Arab Emirates, Qatar, Chile and Thailand accelerate through nationwide fibre and peering upgrades. Top speeds are approaching 250–300 Mbps in 2023–2024 across multiple countries.

Europe placement is consistent in the top twenty: Switzerland, Spain, France, Denmark, Romania, Iceland and Liechtenstein appear frequently, clustering around 180–225 Mbps by 2024, indicating mature broadband markets and investment.

Australia improved over years but still remains outside the top group each year and typically two to four times slower than leaders. Results suggest fibre rollout and geography raising costs for upgrades.

From 2017 to 2024, the world average rises from 31 to 85 Mbps, while Australia grows from 25.5 to 72 Mbps. Australia leads in 2021, matches 2022 and continue growing in 2024 slightly.

Crossovers suggest differences in upgrade timing. Australia leads from major fibre upgrades around 2020, but later improvements were slower than other countries, potentially reflecting investment cycles, density differences and post pandemic traffic growth.

2022 dip likely reflects the Russia–Ukraine war and energy shock disrupting networks along with method changes in the dataset (country coverage, aggregation)

World's yearly average speed vs. Australia

Australian states fixed download speeds (2019–2024)

Every state improves year by year from 2019 to 2024. A clear shift appears around 2021–2023, then another jump in 2024.

Victoria and New South Wales sit near the top across years, while Northern Territory and Tasmania remain lower. Queensland and South Australia stay in the middle. Gaps narrow as speeds rise.

State figures skew higher because they are tests weighted and metro-heavy (more samples from fast urban fibre users). While the global dataset is a country-level average across many slower nations and uses different aggregation, so its mean is lower.

What we found?

  • Australia vs world: 2017–2024 data shows Australia improved but fell behind the world mean again by 2023–24.

  • Leaders gap: Rankings confirm top nations sustain 200–300 Mbps, while Australia remains outside the top-20.

  • GDP vs speed: A clear positive link; Australia sits below peers with similar GDP, indicating unrealised performance.

  • NBN fibre upgrades and investment drove gains: 2024 peaks suggest upgrades reached homes and workplaces.
